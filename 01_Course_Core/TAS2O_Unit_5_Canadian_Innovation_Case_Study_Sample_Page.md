# TAS2O Unit 5 Canadian Innovation Case Study Sample Page

This sample page is intended for binder use as a model classroom handout or discussion page for Unit 5.

Sample case study focus:

- Example innovator: Elsie MacGill, Canadian aeronautical engineer
- Theme: Canadian innovation, design problem solving, and pathways into technological careers
- Secondary emphasis: the role of women in Canadian engineering history and the wider impact of underrepresented voices in technology fields

Case study summary:

Elsie MacGill is a useful example of Canadian innovation because her work shows how engineering decisions respond to practical needs, production constraints, and user requirements. Her career also provides a strong entry point for discussing how women contributed to Canadian technological development in a field where they were historically underrepresented. The case can be used to compare user needs, technical feasibility, workplace constraints, and the larger social value of practical design choices.

Discussion prompts:

- What user need or workplace problem is visible in this case?
- What design or engineering decisions appear to have been shaped by production, safety, or performance requirements?
- How did the innovation affect workers, users, or the wider community?
- Which legal, ethical, social, economic, or environmental considerations may have influenced the work?
- How does this example connect to current Ontario pathways in engineering, manufacturing, and the skilled trades?
- What transferable skills can students identify from the case?

Strand B alignment:

- B1.1: The case can be used to assess the relationship between user needs and technological solutions.
- B1.2: The discussion can be extended to legal, ethical, social, economic, and environmental factors.
- B1.3: The sample highlights a Canadian innovator and opens discussion of women and underrepresented contributors to technology.
- B1.4: The case can be paired with local or Indigenous knowledge perspectives where relevant to broaden the problem-solving lens.

Filing guidance:

- Replace the sample prompts with the actual class discussion notes when the course runs.
- Keep the case study linked to the Strand B verification matrix for binder review.
